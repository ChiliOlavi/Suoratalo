Important Safety Information for Outdoor Areas

At Hotelli Suoratalo, your safety is important to us—even (or especially) when you are just stepping outside for some fresh Imatra air or heading back in after a walk. Please read these practical safety instructions before going outside or using the stairs at our entrance.

Hotel entrance with stairs in winter, snow and sand visible

Be Careful: The Stairs Can Be Slippery!

During the winter and early spring, our outdoor stairs and pathways can get very slippery due to snow, ice, and sometimes a little bit of Finnish weather magic. Our maintenance man, Jari Salonen, works hard to keep the steps clear and safe, shoveling snow and adding sand regularly. Still, the weather can change fast, so please:

  • Always watch your step on the stairs, especially when it's snowy or icy.
  • Hold onto the handrail when going up or down.
  • Use sturdy footwear with good grip.
  • If you notice the stairs are extra slippery or need more sand, please let us know at the reception or tell Jari if you see him around.

General Outdoor Safety Tips

  • Walk slowly and don’t rush, especially if you are carrying luggage or are in a hurry for breakfast.
  • If you are arriving late at night, the outdoor lighting is on—but please take extra care.
  • Children should always be accompanied by an adult outside, especially near the parking area and stairs.

In Case of Accidents

If you do slip or need assistance outside, please come inside to the reception or call us at +358 123 4567. We will help you right away. Anna Korhonen or another staff member will assist you—no need to feel embarrassed, it happens even to the best of us!

Your safety is our priority, but a little bit of Finnish caution goes a long way. We hope you have a pleasant and accident-free stay with us. If you have any questions or spot something we should fix, please don’t hesitate to let us know.
