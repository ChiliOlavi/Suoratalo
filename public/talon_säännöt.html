House Rules – Hotelli Suoratalo

At Hotelli Suoratalo, we believe that a cosy and well-organised environment is best for both our guests and our staff. Our house rules are simple, clear, and designed to make everyone’s stay and workday as pleasant as possible. As a small, family-run hotel, we trust each other to do our part—and sometimes remind each other, too.

Below you’ll find the essential rules for our staff and operations. If you have any questions, please contact hotel manager Matti Virtanen or ask at Reception.

House Rules for Staff

  • Our hotel is a smoke-free workplace. Please respect this for everyone’s comfort.
  • Personal devices: Using personal tablets or watching films (for example, Novigrafia movies) during work hours is not allowed. There is always plenty of work to do.
  • Work devices: RenoTel 720 devices are only for work purposes and only supervisors may use them for hotel business.
  • Shift changes: Please arrive so that you are at your work station, in uniform, when your shift begins. Changing into work clothes before the shift is required.
  • Uniform policy: It is not allowed to move in work areas in your own clothes.
  • Overtime: Overtime must always be approved by your supervisor (Matti Virtanen). Please avoid unnecessary overtime.
  • Duties: Everyone takes care of all the tasks assigned for their own shift. Please do not leave work for the next shift if you have time and ability to complete it.
  • Staff accommodation: Staff are absolutely not allowed to stay overnight in guest rooms, in accordance with city regulations.
  • Doors: Please ensure doors are opened on time, as agreed with Kallon Turvapalvelut security.
  • Atmosphere: TVs should be on, and music at a suitable volume—enough for a nice atmosphere, not too loud.
  • Breakfast area: Keep tables tidy during and after breakfast service.
  • Phone etiquette: Answer the phone promptly and politely. For example:
    "Hotelli Suoratalo, Anna Korhonen speaking, how may I help you?"

If something is unclear, don’t worry—just ask. We are a team, and sometimes Finns forget the rules too.

Guest Information

Our house rules help us ensure a peaceful and comfortable environment for all our guests. For guest-specific information, see Guest Arrival & Stay Information and Important Safety Information.

If you notice anything unusual or need assistance, please let us know at Reception or use our Error Reporting and Troubleshooting page.

Thank you for helping us keep Hotelli Suoratalo a comfortable and safe place for everyone. We appreciate your understanding—even if some rules feel a bit old-fashioned. If you have suggestions or feedback, we are always open to hearing from you.
